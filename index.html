New Data Links Lower Median Household Income, Increasing Health Risks

by JACKSON FREESE

New data outlines the decreasing quality of healthcare, increase of preventable disease for many Americans, as the wealth gap widens.

Obesity
- The data conclusively shows a negative correlation between obesity and median household income. States with higher average household incomes exhibit lower obesity rates across the board. - Poverty directly impacts access to nutrionally impactful food, and makes available calorically dense, cheaper options. - Age and Obesity do not appear to correlate at the state level.

Healthcare
- There exists a strong positive correlation between the percentange of state residents lacking healthcare and those living below the poverty line.
- Data exhibits a negatively correlative relationship between median household income and access to healthcare

J.Freese, Data Analytics Bootcamp, 2021
